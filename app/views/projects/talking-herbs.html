Talking Herbs

We get most of our information about our everyday objects by using our eyes. We look at the size of things, see their colors, read their labels and brands, etc.

This project is a little tweak in our kitchens where pots of spices can make a sound when they are touched. So, instead of grabbing them and reading the label which says the name of the spice, a sound would inform us about what the pot contains. This gag could make people understand the fact that we use our ears to get a huge amount of information from the world around us and how we should pay attention to them. It can also be used as an aid for visually impaired people.
